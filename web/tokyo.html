Halal restaurants in Tokyo: 181 places, trust-levelled by travellers

Tokyo is the easiest city in Japan to eat halal — and it still catches people out. The biggest mistake travellers make is assuming a whole restaurant is halal because a review said so. In Tokyo, it's often one dish, or a few dishes, that are actually halal — the rest of the menu isn't. So be ready to ask the staff about the halal options; they're usually not the most obvious thing on the menu, and asking is normal here.

Some cuisines make it easy: Japanese places doing halal wagyu or ramen, Indian, and Turkish are the most reliable finds. Beyond those, it thins out fast.

That's also why this database works the way it does. We never claim a place is "certified" — every one of the 181 Tokyo places below carries a community trust level, from Reported to Trusted, based on what travellers have reported and confirmed, dish-level caveats included. Where halal food clusters in Tokyo

Tokyo's halal food isn't spread evenly — three areas hold most of it. Every count below comes straight from the live database.

Shin-Okubo & Shinjuku 新大久保・新宿

43 places in Shinjuku ward

The densest halal corridor in the city. Shin-Okubo is Tokyo's Koreatown, and the streets between it and Shinjuku station hold the biggest concentration of halal restaurants and grocers anywhere in Tokyo — Japanese yakiniku and ramen alongside Malaysian, Indian and Central Asian kitchens. If food is a priority for your trip, basing yourself within a couple of stops of Shinjuku means you're never far from a solid option, including late in the evening when much of the city has already closed.

Asakusa & Ueno 浅草・上野 — Taitō ward 台東区

49 places in Taitō ward

The highest count of any ward — and conveniently, it's the one you'll probably be sightseeing in anyway. Around Sensō-ji and the Asakusa backstreets, halal Japanese food is at its easiest: wagyu yakiniku, sukiyaki and ramen made without pork or alcohol, plus cafes used to fielding questions in English. Ueno's park and market streets add more options a few minutes away. This is the area for eating a halal version of the classic Tokyo dishes mid-sightseeing, rather than making a detour for dinner.

  • Sekai Cafe Asakusa 世界カフェ 浅草 · Cafe
  • Naritaya Halal Ramen 成田屋 ハラールラーメン · Japanese
  • Halal Wagyu Yakiniku Panga · Wagyu
  • Alibaba Silk Road Uyghur Cuisine · Uyghur

Shibuya 渋谷

26 places in Shibuya ward

Fewer places than Shinjuku or Asakusa, but reliable anchors right in the middle of everything — a cluster of halal wagyu and ramen spots around Center Gai and Dōgenzaka, and kebab standbys toward Harajuku. Useful when you're already there for the crossing, the shopping or a night out, rather than a destination in itself. Check the trust level and the dish-level notes before you commit, and expect to ask the staff which menu items are the halal ones.

  • Turkish Restaurant Ankara — Dogenzaka · Turkish
  • GYUMON Center Gai · Wagyu
  • Kuta Bali Cafe Harajuku · Indonesian
  • Aladdin Harajuku Kebab · Kebab

How trust levels work

Every place carries a trust level from 1 to 4. Reported: a traveller or public source says there are halal options — start here, and ask. Community-verified: at least three travellers have confirmed it on the ground. Photo-verified: a traveller has uploaded evidence, like a halal-only menu. Trusted: backed by a recognised certification body. We never certify anything ourselves — the levels surface what travellers have actually checked, dish-level caveats included.
