# Competitive Analysis

## Market Overview

The Muslim travel market is valued at $200B+ and growing. Halal food discovery is a core pain point, yet no app has achieved dominance — the space is fragmented with lifestyle apps that bolt on food as a secondary feature.

## Direct Competitors

### Zabihah

- **What it is:** Oldest Halal restaurant directory (founded 1998)
- **Strengths:** Large database in North America, strong brand recognition in the Muslim community, established user-contributed data
- **Weaknesses:** Dated UI/UX, minimal international coverage, no real verification beyond user reviews, no map provider flexibility, web-first not mobile-first
- **Coverage:** Strong in USA/Canada, weak elsewhere

### HalalTrip

- **What it is:** Muslim travel platform — restaurants, hotels, prayer times
- **Strengths:** Good Southeast Asia coverage, comprehensive travel features, partnerships with tourism boards
- **Weaknesses:** Thin coverage in East Asia and Africa, no crowdsource incentive system, relies entirely on Google Maps
- **Coverage:** Strong in Malaysia, Singapore, Indonesia; weak in China, Japan, Korea

### Muslim Pro

- **What it is:** Dominant Muslim lifestyle app (150M+ downloads) — prayer times, Quran, qibla, + restaurant finder
- **Strengths:** Massive user base, brand trust, one-stop-shop for Muslim daily needs
- **Weaknesses:** Halal food is a minor feature, restaurant data is sparse and often outdated, no contributor system, no verification
- **Coverage:** Global for prayer times; restaurant data is inconsistent everywhere

### Halal Navi

- **What it is:** Japan-focused Halal restaurant finder
- **Strengths:** Deep coverage in Japan, understands the specific needs of Muslim travellers in a non-Muslim country, good UI
- **Weaknesses:** Japan-only, small team, limited scalability
- **Coverage:** Japan only

### CrescentRating

- **What it is:** Muslim-friendly travel rating and advisory platform
- **Strengths:** Credibility with tourism boards and governments, GMTI (Global Muslim Travel Index) is an industry benchmark
- **Weaknesses:** More B2B than consumer-facing, not a restaurant finder, no real-time crowdsourced data
- **Coverage:** Ratings/advisory — not a place discovery app

## Geographic Blind Spots — Our Opportunity

No existing app provides reliable **worldwide** coverage. Every competitor is strong in one region and weak everywhere else. Key underserved areas:

### China (largest gap)
- Google Maps is blocked/inaccurate in China (GCJ-02 coordinate offset)
- China has **widespread Halal food infrastructure** — Hui Muslim restaurants (清真) in virtually every city, Uyghur restaurants, Lanzhou beef noodle shops (兰州拉面) everywhere
- AMap and Baidu Maps have this data but are entirely in Chinese
- No existing Halal app integrates Chinese map providers

### East Asia (Japan, South Korea)
- Halal Navi covers Japan but nothing else; Korea is essentially uncovered
- Growing Muslim tourism but fragmented information

### Europe
- Large Muslim populations in France, UK, Germany, Netherlands — Halal food exists but is hard for travellers to discover
- No centralized, verified directory for travellers (locals rely on word of mouth)

### Central Asia & Africa
- Google Maps has limited POI data in many areas
- Significant Muslim populations with rich food scenes invisible to travellers
- Regional map providers (e.g., Yandex for Central Asia) may be more accurate

### Latin America
- Small but growing Muslim communities; virtually zero Halal discovery tools

**Our approach:** be the single global app that works everywhere, adapting to regional map providers and data sources where standard tools fall short.

## Key Takeaway

The market has no app that is simultaneously:
1. **Halal-food-focused** (not a lifestyle app with food bolted on)
2. **Truly global** (works worldwide, not just strong in one region)
3. **Traveller-first** (adaptive maps, offline, dual-language addresses)
4. **Verified and trusted** (tiered verification, contributor incentives)
5. **Resilient to regional restrictions** (adapts map providers where Google Maps falls short)

This is our lane.
